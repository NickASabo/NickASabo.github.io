Invasive Species

General Overview
An invasive species is a non-native organism which disrupts an ecosystem. This disruption, generally threatening the extinction of a native organism, may be habitat loss, competition for an essential resource, predation, pollution, or radiation poisoning. Such organisms may be particularly problematic as the carrier of harmful parasites or when they lack a natural predator in the region.

History
Originally defined by ancient scholastics to describe the intercontinental, human-transported spread of macro-organisms, this definition was broadened in the middle 3000's by, again, the now-defunct Fantastical College of Mars to include interplanetary spread and micro-organisms. As the spread of micro-organisms is nigh-inevitable, people stopped caring by the late-middle 3000's.

Modern Environmentalism
As the Federated Republic of Planets spread beyond their local sector into planets not terraformed, but blessed with life of their own, the topic of invasive species re-entered the public consciousness. As such, strict embargos are in effect. Ecologists are required on every exploratory mission to identify keystone species, classify niches, and collect reconstructable samples, and crewmen and mimes must obtain all necessary materials from the host planet.

Invasive Species in Unnatural Environments
Ecologists recently introduced the idea of invasive species in sentient-created environs, such as spaceships or nurseries. For instance, on the Ashbery, after the first expedition to Skaoi IV, an astrobiologist brought on board six (6) specimens of Ghallas Ratbird, which began displacing the local populace of Felis Postneodomesticus owned by the esteemed Director of Travels and Communication. The Ratbirds:
(1) Filled the cat's litterboxes, and the rest of the ship, with guano, overtaking and polluting the cat's habitat,
(2) Introduced a secondary invasive species of flea,
(3) Stole the cat's Fatty-Cockroach Delight, their favorite.

Such specimens were released by raving lunatics converted during the visit to Skaoi IV, now properly quarantined. Nonetheless, unless this infestation is dealt with quickly, a certain astrobiologist will have hell to pay.

Kelsey Khan, Director of Travels and Communications, The Ashbery, Federated Republic of Planets
