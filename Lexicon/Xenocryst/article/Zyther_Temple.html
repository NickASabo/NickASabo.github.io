You are members of the Carroll and Ogden research teams, freshly returned from simultaneous first expeditions to Skaoi IV, now aboard The Ashbery, a research travel hub for the Federated Republic of Planets.

Zyther Temple

A large cylindrical, temple on the western continent of Skaoi IV at the base of a mountain. The structure of the temple itself is rather unimpressive; the interesting thing about this temple is that is floating 1.3 meter off the surface of the planet. The temple stands 40 meters tall, 800 meters in diameter, and is composed completely out of Titanite. The inside of the temple is broken into three floors. There is a main worship chamber that is 100 meters cylinder extending through all three floors. The floors are built to open face the main chamber. Various rooms and offices line the exterior of the temple. There are no seats in the main worship chamber, only a single column, presumed, to be an altar. Small disks of Titanite were found floating around the temple. We believe the Skaoi IV people to use the disks as floating seats. A pool of red liquid was found in a chamber near the main hall labeled Kila. Six other smaller cylindrical temples float around the main temple. The six buildings rotate around the main temple synced with the time of day.

The temple is devoted to the worship of Zyther, the God of Floating Rocks. Various tomes describing ritualistic dancing were found in the office of the building. We believe dancing is the main worship technique for the Zyther Religion. Zyther seemed to be a kind deity, giving his followers the rocks to float on to avoid sitting on the ground. The temple seemed abandoned quickly as many spaces seemed prepared for some sort of service.

Keith Anderson, Intern
